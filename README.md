Project :- Digital Chemistry Lab Website

Objective :-  

1. To create an informative and professional research lab website.
2. To highlight the lab’s mission, vision, and research focus.
3. To provide details about ongoing research areas in computational chemistry and AI.
4. To showcase lab innovations like Spectral-Hub, Unmix-X, Decode-X, Predict-X.
5. To provide access to lab publications and update them automatically.
6. To encourage collaborations from industry, academia, and students.
7. To provide a direct communication channel through contact form/email.

Summary :- 

1. The Digital Chemistry Lab Website is an academic research-based website designed for the University of Mumbai Digital Chemistry Lab. 
2. The purpose of this website is to showcase the lab’s work in Computational Chemistry, Artificial Intelligence in Chemistry, Spectral Data Intelligence, and Process Automation.
3. The website presents research activities, ongoing projects, lab facilities, publications, team members, and collaboration opportunities.
4. It also highlights the lab’s key innovation platform Spectral-Hub, which provides AI-powered chemical insights from spectral data.
5. This platform acts as a professional research portal for students, researchers, and industry collaborators.

Key Features of Website :- 

1. Modern academic research lab website layout
2. Research areas explained with cards and details
3. Projects section for lab innovations
4. Team and collaborator showcase
5. Facilities and tools overview
6. Publication auto-updating from Google Scholar
7. Collaboration section for industry and academics
8. Contact form for communication

Conclusion - 

1. The Acute Heart Disease Prediction Model successfully predicts whether a patient is at risk of AHD using clinical health features like age, cholesterol, blood pressure, chest pain type, and heart rate.
2. Data preprocessing (handling missing values, encoding categorical features, and scaling) improved the overall model reliability and consistency.
3. Feature scaling and feature engineering helped in improving the model’s learning ability and reduced bias caused by different feature ranges.
4. The Decision Tree Classifier provided an interpretable model, making it easier to understand how predictions are made in a healthcare context.
5. Hyperparameter tuning using GridSearchCV improved model performance and helped select the best tree depth and splitting criteria for accurate classification.

Result - 

1. The final Decision Tree model achieved strong classification performance after optimization using GridSearchCV.
2. The tuned model showed improved accuracy and better generalization compared to the default Decision Tree model.
3. Evaluation metrics such as Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-score confirmed that the model can effectively detect heart disease risk.
4. The confusion matrix results indicated that the model correctly classified most patients into heart disease and non-heart disease categories.
5. Overall, the system can be used as an early screening tool to support doctors in identifying high-risk patients for further medical diagnosis.
